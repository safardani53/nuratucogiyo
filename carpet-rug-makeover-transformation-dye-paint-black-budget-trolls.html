I painted my carpet because I wanted a makeover on a budget - I dont care if Im trolled, more

A WOMAN, who decided to take redecorating her hallway into her own hands, has shared the process she went through to dye her grey-cream carpets black.

Instead of opting to buy a brand new carpet for her home, Linzi (on TikTok as @linziaxk664), set about transforming the floors with liquid fabric dye and colour boost from Scratch Doctor.

Lining her gear up in order, she got her Henry the hoover, a paintbrush, a carpet scraper, bucket, and her two dyes ready for the task. 

“Decided to paint my hallway carpet!!” she wrote over this. 

Dumping both bottles of of Scratch Doctor liquids into the bucket, she then took her paintbrush and began coating her carpet in the dye, layer by layer. 

“Has taken me an hour and cost £20,” she told viewers as she spread the liquid around.

Linzi then unveiled the first section that she managed to get done, revealing that she managed to cover the area at the bottom of the stairs up to the opposite wall. 

A second photo showed an update, where she’d managed to cover the majority of the carpet in the black dye without issue. 

“Not bad,” she wrote over this with a smiling emoji, “Just had to try it!” 

People flocked to the comments section to share their thoughts on her carpet transformation, with many arguing that she should’ve bought a new carpet instead of going to this effort.

“I’d just buy a new carpet,” one person wrote with a laughing emoji.

Linzi was quick to fire back as she replied: “If more people decided to use this method 90% of old carpets wouldn’t end up in landfills.”

Others, however, came to her defence as one hit back: “Many people can’t afford new carpets. For £20 this is a good way to cover nasty stains that won't come out. Well done."

Another said: “Won’t be doing this but doesn’t look too bad well done."

A third shared that she hadn’t “dyed a carpet but have had a huge rug professionally dyed. The rug is still going strong 5 years later."

While a fourth added: “love the idea, do you have to wash it to get excess dye out and how many bottles did you use for how big of a space?"

Linzi replied: “I used only the bottles in the video. Would like to second coat it but happy with the results to leave it for now. Doesn’t need washing out.”
