Celebrity Weddings of 2021

April 2024 · 49 minute read

Alexia Echevarria & Todd Nepola

The Real Housewives of Miami star said "I do" to her new husband in a Dec. 16 ceremony in St. Barths at the Eden Rock hotel, she announced on Instagram.

"This year has been a whirlwind to say the least, but finishing it off as Mr. & Mrs. gives me hope for the years to come. Thank you for all your kind wishes and love. 12.16.21," Echevarria captioned a slideshow of photos from her special day and the seaside ceremony.

Will Forte & Olivia Modling

The Saturday Night Live alum wed his fiancée on July 31 in a surprise ceremony, PEOPLE exclusively revealed on Dec. 8.

The couple, who welcomed 10-month-old daughter Zoe on Feb. 15, initially planned to wait out the pandemic before moving ahead with wedding plans.

Ultimately, while they were in New Mexico filming Forte's new comedy series MacGruber, they decided to pull together a spur-of-the-moment ceremony in just two weeks to surprise his parents.

"And we just had this really fun, delightful wedding, pretty small, in the back of Jorma Taccone's house." (Taccone is one of the three main MacGruber creators.)

"It's fun to say, 'Wife,'" added Forte. "I've never worn any jewelry in my life and it's fun to wear a wedding ring. It already felt like we were married before. It doesn't feel a lot different, but it's still fun and exciting to say, 'Wife.'"

Ty Pennington & Kellee Merrell

The Trading Spaces alum tied the knot with his fiancée, a social media manager originally from Vancouver, in an intimate ceremony with just eight guests on Nov. 27 in Savannah, Georgia.

"I never thought I would get married. It wasn't a priority. But this incredible person came into my life and I just knew I couldn't let her go," Ty told PEOPLE of finding "the one."

The small nuptials took place at the 19th-century home the couple is restoring together.

Paris Hilton & Carter Reum

The entrepreneur and DJ said "I do" to her venture capitalist fiancé in an extravagant fairytale wedding at the Bel-Air estate of Hilton's late grandfather, Barron Hilton, on Nov. 11, a source confirmed to PEOPLE.

The bride and groom exchanged vows before family and friends including Kim Kardashian West, Kyle Richards and Paula Abdul.

"My forever begins today... ✨💍 11/11 💝," Hilton captioned a photo of herself in her wedding gown, adding the hashtags #JustMarried and #ForeverHiltonReum.

The heiress' wedding festivities lasted all weekend, including multiple music performances from the likes of Kim Petras and Demi Lovato, as well as two parties to celebrate her nuptials, all of which were captured on camera for the reality star's new docuseries, Paris in Love.

Simon Huck & Phil Riportella

The PR executive and close friend of the Kardashian family tied the knot with his longtime love on Nov. 13 in a private ceremony.

The pair's star-studded nuptials were held at the Bel-Air Hotel, with famous faces in attendance including Khloe Kardashian, Kendall Jenner, Kim Kardashian, Malika Haqq, Tan France, Chrissy Teigen and John Legend and more.

The newlyweds got engaged in January 2020, when Huck popped the question in a romantic beachside proposal in Montauk, New York.

Elisabeth Röhm & Peter Glatzer

The Law & Order alum married her fiancé on Oct. 5 in an intimate ceremony held in the backyard of their Los Angeles home, they told The New York Times. Röhm, 48, also shared photos from the special day on Instagram.

"So… I have some news!" the actress wrote. "Beyond happy and blessed to be married to the wonderful Peter Glatzer @glatzerama #wegotmarried."

Their intimate, 30-minute ceremony was officiated by a friend, the pair told the Times, and was followed by a Champagne toast and a meal Röhm likened to "a Thanksgiving dinner." They're planning to celebrate with more family and friends — around 150 of them – in January, they added.

Renee Montgomery & Sirena Grace

One year after they made their relationship official, the Atlanta Dream co-owner and vice president quietly wed the singer in April 2020, the pair revealed to PEOPLE.

After Montgomery popped the question during a trip to France with friends in Nov. 2019, the pair's dream wedding plans were put on hold when the pandemic hit in 2020. But ultimately, they decided on an impromptu ceremony to mark their one-year anniversary.

"We literally just woke up that morning and said, 'Oh, it's April 20. We're not going to get married.' And then we're like, 'Well, do you still want to do it? We could probably do it. Let's just try.' So it just happened like that, there wasn't a lot of planning," Grace told PEOPLE.

Ivy Love Getty & Tobias Alexander Engel

The model, who is the great-granddaughter of oil tycoon J. Paul Getty, married her photographer fiancé in a star-studded ceremony officiated by House Speaker Nancy Pelosi on Nov. 6.

The wedding, which was held at City Hall in San Francisco, was part of a weekend full of celebrations, which included a 1960s-themed bash at the Palace of Fine Arts in San Francisco ahead of the nuptials.

Getty and Engel first met through a family member of the heiress, and later reconnected at Paris Fashion Week when he was taking photos of her at an event, she told Vogue.

Malala Yousafzai & Asser Malik

On Nov. 9, the human rights activist revealed on Instagram that she and her fiancé had wed in an intimate ceremony in Birmingham, England.

"Today marks a precious day in my life. Asser and I tied the knot to be partners for life," she wrote alongside a series of photos from the happy day.

"We celebrated a small nikkah ceremony at home in Birmingham with our families. Please send us your prayers," added the Nobel Peace Prize winner, 24. "We are excited to walk together for the journey ahead."

The nikkah is a Muslim marriage ceremony that often takes place in a mosque or at the bride's home, according to Brides.

Jason Gotay & Michael Hartung

The Gossip Girl star said "I do" to his fiancé in an "elevated backyard ceremony" on Oct. 25 in Memphis, New York.

For the intimate ceremony, the pair — who met in 2014 while working together on NBC's Peter Pan Live — wore matching white leather Dr. Martens sneakers instead of dress shoes.

"Our vision was ivory suits that would reference the traditional color worn by a bride, and allow us to stand out among the lush, green surroundings and our families' deep, rich, fall color scheme," the pair told Brides. "We were styled by our dear friend and brilliant artist Stephanie Tricola, who helped us put together a monochromatic look starring suits by Hugo Boss. Modern touches like a white leather Dr. Martens sneaker helped the look feel fresh and contemporary while staying formal and sophisticated."

Tarek El Moussa & Heather Rae Young

The Flipping 101 host flipped the Selling Sunset star's last name! After a year-long engagement, the couple wed on Oct. 23 near Santa Barbara, California, surrounded by family and friends.

"We're best friends, we're true soulmates, and our love is so rare and so special," Young told PEOPLE exclusively. "It's the love I've always dreamed of my whole life."

El Moussa agreed, adding, "I'm just excited to live this life with her. We've got the two babies, we have a family, and we have a very bright future."

Jesse McCartney & Katie Peterson

The singer found his "Beautiful Soul"! McCartney married the actress in Carmel, California on Oct. 23. Tying the knot in a heartfelt ceremony, their dog Bailey even adorably served as their flower girl.

Ahead of the wedding, McCartney told PEOPLE he could hardly wait for the big day.

"I think that moment of settling in at the altar and just watching her come down is going to be pretty special and having our families there and witnessing all of it… It's hard not to be romantic about that portion of the night," he explained.

Meadow Walker & Louis Thornton-Allan

Paul Walker's daughter is a married woman. The model married Louis Thornton-Allan in an intimate wedding in the Dominican Republic in October.

In a touching tribute, the bride walked down the aisle with her godfather Vin Diesel, her late father's close friend and Fast and Furious costar.

Freida Pinto & Cory Tran

Surprise! During an October appearance on The Kelly Clarkson Show, the actress revealed that she and her photographer fiancé eloped during quarantine

"When we got engaged we thought we'd have this most magical wedding," Pinto explained. "But then COVID happened and it's still happening and we just realized we'd be planning this for the rest of our lives and never doing it. So we decided one day to go to the Honda Center in Anaheim [California]."

"It was absolutely perfect. We got married and then we got to go home and take an afternoon nap," she shared.

Over the summer, the couple announced they were expecting their first child. Pinto told fans that "baby Tran" was due in the fall.

In an Oct. 21 Instagram post, the Slumdog Millionaire star posted photos from the big day to celebrate their one-year anniversary.

"@coryt and I believe so much in balancing spontaneity with just the right amount of planning. One day it felt so right and so genuinely aligned that we decided we wanted to make it official," she wrote. "This felt so special and fun and let's be honest… it reflected the time in our world just perfectly!"

Sarah Levy & Graham Outerbridge

The Schitt's Creek alum married the actor and producer in October, sharing a strip of photo booth pics to confirm the happy news.

"Bells are ringing ✨✨10.16.2021" Levy wrote.

The couple has been together since at least 2018, with Outerbridge frequently celebrating his now-wife on social media. When Schitt's Creek wrapped after six seasons in April 2020, he raved about her role as Twyla Sands.

"Words can't describe how proud I am of this wonderful woman," he wrote on Instagram, posting a photo of Levy in character. "She's the most talented and graceful person I've met, and I'm luckier than I could ever imagine! I love you @sarahplevy! Twyla was such a gift!"

Anne Burrell & Stuart Claxton

The Food Network star wed Stuart Claxton on Oct. 16 in a ceremony at Windridge Estates Redbarn 20 in Cazenovia, N.Y., where Burrell grew up.

Speaking exclusively to PEOPLE ahead of her wedding, Burrell, 52, opened up about her nuptials after getting engaged in April 2020 while quarantining with her family and Claxton in Cazenovia.

"I can't believe it's really here. It's kind of surreal," she said of the ceremony. "We feel so much love and support from everyone who's coming."

Famous faces in attendance included fellow chefs Rachael Ray (who was a bridesmaid) and Alex Guarnaschelli, as well as Marc Murphy, Jeff Mauro, and Jenni "J Woww" Farley.

Roger Waters & Kamilah Chavis

The Pink Floyd musician wed his longtime girlfriend, he announced on Oct. 13. "I'm so happy, finally a keeper," he captioned a series of photos of the pair celebrating their wedding day.

"A very happy man," he wrote alongside a solo shot, adding, "A very beautiful bride" on a photo of Chavis.

This is Waters' fifth marriage; he most recently split from actress and director Laurie Durning in 2015 after three years of marriage, according to Page Six.

Waters spoke about his relationship with Chavis in a 2018 interview with Argentinian outlet Infobae, sharing at the time that the couple had already been together for several years after first meeting when Chavis was working in transportation and ended up driving the musician to a number of shows.

Sarah Snook & Dave Lawson

The Succession star revealed to Vogue Australia that she wed her longtime friend, comedian Dave Lawson, in February after quarantining with him at the start of the COVID-19 pandemic.

"At the beginning of the pandemic last year, I got locked down in Melbourne with one of my best mates and we fell in love," she said in the outlet's November cover story.

She added that the pair had been close friends for years before their relationship turned romantic, explaining, "We've been friends since 2014, lived together, traveled together, always excited to see each other, but totally platonic."

Jennifer Gates & Nayel Nassar

Jennifer, the eldest daughter of billionaire Microsoft co-founder Bill Gates and Melinda French Gates, wed the Olympic equestrian on Oct. 16.

The pair tied the knot in Westchester County, New York, PEOPLE confirmed.

Gates, 25, and Nassar, 30, got engaged in January 2020.

Meghan King & Cuffe Owens

The Real Housewives alum is officially Mrs. Meghan King Owens. The star wed Joe Biden and Jill Biden's nephew on Oct. 11 at Owens' parents' home in Kennett Square, Pennsylvania.

The president and first lady also attended the "small, family wedding," and returned to D.C. that afternoon, according to the White House.

"Our wedding was about two things for us," King told Brides magazine. "Our love for and commitment to each other, and our family — each of our families, and the new very large and very tight family that we were bonding together by getting married. That's it."

Owens is a Los Angeles-based attorney and the son of the president's sister and close adviser, Valerie Owens, and her husband, Jack.

King appeared on three seasons of Real Housewives of Orange County and is mom to daughter Aspen and twin sons Hart and Hayes.

Adam Devine & Chloe Bridges

The Pitch Perfect star and his longtime love are married, a rep for the actor confirmed to PEOPLE on Oct. 10.

Devine's Workaholics costar Erik Griffin celebrated the news on Instagram, sharing a photo of himself with the groom as well as costars Anders Holm and Blake Anderson. "Dem Boyz! Devine is off the market! Such an awesome wedding!" Griffin captioned the shot.

Christina Ricci & Mark Hampton

The Addams Family actress tied the knot with the celebrity hairdresser, she revealed on Instagram on Oct. 9.

"Mr. and Mrs. 💒♥️💒♥️💒," she captioned a sweet selfie of the couple together. Hampton shared the same photo on his Instagram, adding the caption, "#justmarried 🥰❤️💒❤️🥰"

Their nuptials come two months after the actress revealed that the pair are expecting their first child together.

Matt Fraser & Alexa Papigiotis

The psychic medium Matt Fraser and his girlfriend of nearly five years, Alexa Papigiotis, said "I do" in an intimate ceremony on Oct. 9 in Palm Beach, Florida.

The Meet the Frasers couple wed at the historic Henry Morrison Flagler Museum, in front of nearly 100 loved ones.

The pair first met in January 2017 and got engaged in May 2019. The pair tell PEOPLE that ultimately, having to postpone their wedding due to COVID-19 wasn't all bad as it gave them more time to plan.

"It allowed us to give everyone time to get vaccinated," Fraser said. "But then at the same time, you need every minute that you can to plan and to prep, like it's literally taken us two years to plan this wedding."

Keshia Knight Pulliam & Brad James

On Oct. 2, The Cosby Show star revealed on Instagram that she had tied the knot with her actor fiancé in late September.

"Last week my life forever changed … I married my best friend at our home surrounded by family & it was magical!!!" she captioned a series of photos of her intimate nuptials.

The ceremony "wasn't the large destination wedding we were originally planning," the actress shared, adding, "Instead, it was intimate, whimsical, full of love, laughter and joy… Absolutely perfect!!"

Calling her husband her "soulmate," she added in the caption, "Thank you for renewing my vulnerability & faith in unconditionally love ❤️ #TheJamesBond."

Tiera & Kamren Kennedy

The country singer wed her creative director fiancé on Oct. 2 at Southall Meadows in Franklin, Tennessee, PEOPLE confirmed exclusively.

Tiera, 23, and Kennedy, 25, first met in 2014, after Kennedy slid into her DMs to discuss a camera she wanted to buy. The pair having been dating for seven years.

"We just clicked from the very beginning," the "Found It In You" singer told PEOPLE. "We loved working together and hanging out together and were best friends from that very first FaceTime."

Lena Dunham & Luis Felber

The Girls creator is married! Dunham, 35, said "I do" with musician boyfriend Luis Felber, also 35, on Sept. 25, a source confirmed to PEOPLE.

The pair wed in front of approximately 60 of their loved ones at Soho's Union Club in London, according to Vogue, and the nuptials saw Dunham done up in three custom Christopher Kane dresses.

Among the nine people in the actress's bridal party were pals Taylor Swift and Tommy Dorfman.

Kyle Cooke & Amanda Batula

The Summer House stars tied the knot in an outdoor ceremony in Batula's backyard in New Jersey on Sept. 25.

The two said "I do" in front of several of their Summer House costars, including Paige DeSorbo and Carl Radke, who served as a bridesmaid and groomsman, respectively.

The pair, who are also partners at their sparkling hard tea company, Loverboy, got engaged in 2018. Their relationship has been followed throughout the hit Bravo show over the years.

Freddie Highmore

On the Sept. 27 episode of Jimmy Kimmel Live!, the Good Doctor star revealed that he recently tied the knot.

"Yes, I got married," he said, after Kimmel pointed out his wedding band. "It's funny — ever since I've been wearing this ring, people have been asking me if I'm married, so I figured I should clarify it."

"I'm not going to jump up and down on the couch on the talk show and express my excitement that way," Highmore said, referencing Tom Cruise's infamous 2005 Oprah appearance. "I know that you do that in America. But I'm as happy as a Brit can be and I'm married to a very wonderful woman now. So yes, I feel very happy."

Molly Bernard & Hannah Lieberman

The Younger star said "I do" to her fiancée, she revealed on her Instagram on Sept. 24.

"WIVES! Perfect day, perfect party," Bernard captioned a photo of the brides at their nuptials, adding credits to photographer Ro Agents-Juska for the candid shot and florist Robin Rose Hilleary of Fleurotica for supplying the flowers at the celebration.

"WHAT A SURREAL JOY TO BE MARRIED TO MY TREASURE!" added the actress, who geotagged The Boathouse in Brooklyn, New York's Prospect Park as the location of the ceremony.

Elle McLemore & Josh Levinson

After a five-year engagement, the Army Wives star and the celebrity tennis coach are married.

"We've been best friends since we were 11 years old," Levinson, 30, told PEOPLE in an exclusive interview. "We grew up together and I've been in love with her ever since we were kids. Although I had to dig myself out of that treacherous friend zone! But she's always been the love of my life. She's just my best friend and on every level we are just perfect."

The pair tied the knot on Sept. 25 at Restoration Oaks — a 950-acre family-owned blueberry ranch just north of Santa Barbara.

Inbar Lavi & Dan Bar Shira

On Sept. 13, the Lucifer actress wed her fiancé in a bohemian beachside ceremony at the Al Hayam in their native Israel.

"We're both Israeli and very connected to our roots," Lavi told Brides. "Our family lives here and we were raised by the Mediterranean, which made us who we are today."

The pair incorporated several cultural traditions into their nuptials, including a chuppah on the beach, which the bride called "a bit traditional, and a bit untraditional." In addition to cultural inspiration, the bride and groom also paid homage to Burning Man, the desert festival where they met in August 2019, with light shows and DJs.

Lucia Aniello & Paul W. Downs

The Hacks co-creators — and newly minted Emmy winners — tied the knot in Italy recently.

Hannah Einbinder, an actress on the show, shared the happy news with Vanity Fair ahead of the 73rd Primetime Emmy Awards. She noted that she traveled to the wedding, as the cast and crew had grown close during filming.

In accepting her Emmy for outstanding directing for a comedy series, Aniello gushed to her new husband: "I'm in love with you, which is why I married you last weekend!"

The couple previously told The Hollywood Reporter that they first met in 2007, in an improv class at the Upright Citizens Brigade.

Jasmine Tookes & Juan David Borrero

The supermodel and Borrero tied the knot in his native Ecuador on Sept. 4.

For the ceremony, which was attended by a number of friends and fellow models, Tookes wore a hand-embellished floral lace Zuhair Murad gown with long sleeves and a high collar.

"We both wanted to be as classic and traditional as we could with our outfits," the model told Vogue.

"Still can't believe I'm living in a real life fairytale with you @juandavidborrero ❤️ 9.4.21. I'm officially Mrs. Borrero ✨" Tookes wrote on Instagram alongside several stunning shots from the big day, including one of the pair standing at the altar and walking down the aisle.

Clinton Moxam & Uche Nwosu

The Are You the One? stars, who first met on the sixth season of the MTV dating show, tied the knot on Sept. 4 in Chicago.

The pair said "I do" surrounded by family and friends, including a number of their former AYTO costars, some of whom shared snapshots from the wedding on social media.

The pair got engaged in June 2020, three years after they first began dating.

Jameson Rodgers & Sarah Allison Turner

The country singers wed at The Venue at Birchwood in Spring Hill, Tennessee on Sept. 5, PEOPLE confirmed exclusively.

"I've never felt more loved than I did last Sunday with all our friends and family in one place celebrating Sarah and me. I can't wait to spend the rest of my life with Sarah," Rodgers gushed to PEOPLE.

"As cliché as it might sound, I've really just always known [he was the one]. We were friends for years so by the time we got together there was no question!" Turner told PEOPLE. The pair met 10 years ago at a songwriting workshop at Nashville's iconic Bluebird Café, and began dating six years ago.

Brit Taylor & Adam Chaffings

The country musicians wed in an intimate ceremony in early September, they told PEOPLE exclusively.

"As two performers who have been putting on shows for people from an early age, the last thing we wanted was for our wedding to be a big production," Taylor explained. "We love putting on shows for people. But this show [was] just for us."

The couple, who wanted their wedding to be "super simple" and out of the ordinary, said "I do" at a trailer park at Hotel Luna Mystica in Taos, New Mexico, with only three guests beside them.

After their honeymoon, they returned to Nashville for a Sept. 13 reception to celebrate with friends and family.

Mark Ronson & Grace Gummer

Four months after getting engaged, the music producer and the actress tied the knot, Ronson revealed on his 46th birthday.

"To my truest love...out of nowhere, you made 45 hands down the greatest year of my life. And I'm sure it took me 45 years to become the man worthy of your love," Ronson wrote on Sept. 4, sharing a black-and-white photo of the couple holding hands at their nuptials.

"I hope I spend every one of these birthdays by your side til my last day. And beyond. Forever and ever yours ❤️❤️❤️❤️❤️❤️ (and yes, we got married)," he confirmed in the caption.

This is the second marriage for both Ronson and Gummer.

Maddison McKibbin & Chelsea Hayes

Professional beach volleyball player and The Amazing Race finalist McKibbin married Hayes on Aug. 28 in Mexico.

For the "extremely casual" nuptials, the groom wore a white linen shirt and made the playlist himself. Guests were greeted by a mariachi band playing, and the couple opted for no assigned seating and a buffet of Mexican food, including popsicles rather than a wedding cake for dessert.

"It's more about people getting together, seeing old friends and having a good time," McKibbin tells PEOPLE.

Camila Banus & Marlon Aquino

The Days of Our Lives actress married longtime partner Aquino at The Castle House Estate located in Joshua Tree, California, over the Aug. 28 weekend.

"We picked this venue because it was so different and unique," Banus told PEOPLE exclusively. "It felt like an oasis in the middle of the desert. The minute we saw it, we knew it was for us."

Banus and Aquino couldn't "wait to commemorate a decade of being together with a romantic and unforgettable evening in Joshua Tree," the actress added.

Amy Roloff & Chris Marek

On Aug. 28, the Little People, Big World star and Marek said "I do" in front of 146 of their closest family and friends at Roloff Farms in Hillsboro, Oregon.

During the ceremony, which took place outside in front of a gazebo, the bride stunned in a dress from Justin Alexander's Sincerity Collection, while Marek wore a black suit as they exchanged their vows.

The pair's nuptials were officiated by Michael Williams and also saw Roloff walk down the aisle to "Heart to Heart," which was composed by Marek's best man, Rick Hinkes.

Will Kopelman & Alexandra Michler

The art consultant, who split from Drew Barrymore five years ago, married Vogue's director of fashion development over the weekend of Aug. 28 in Massachusetts.

Kopelman and Barrymore's two daughters, Olive, 8, and Frankie, 7, acted as flower girls during the ceremony, as shown by Kopelman's sister Jill Kargman, who shared sweet photos from the event on Instagram.

In other photos from the celebration, the newly wedded pair are seen exchanging vows surrounded by a gorgeous floral arrangement with the ocean in the background. Kopelman wore a navy suit for the occasion, while his bride wore a beautiful white wedding gown.

Vanessa Grimaldi & Josh Wolfe

The Bachelor alumna said "I do" to her businessman fiancé in front of the couple's immediate family on Aug. 20 in their hometown of Montreal, Canada, PEOPLE confirmed exclusively.

"I'm over the moon!" Grimaldi gushed. "Josh is the best partner to have in life and I feel so grateful."

The pair, who have been together for two years and got engaged last summer, tied the knot at Le Mount Stephen Hotel, in an event planned by Tania Tassone.

C) Then I took a few flicks with Somebody's Husband," she joked in the caption. "Big thanks to @whiteedenweddings for being so gracious and accommodating and making this feel so real and special."

Gabriel Jagger & Anouk Winzenried

Jagger, son of Mick Jagger, wed Swiss socialite Winzenried over the July 24 weekend. The couple released an official portrait of the seemingly quiet ceremony in London.

The portrait appears to be the only public image of the ceremony, which is typical of the couple, who tends to keep their relationship under wraps.

Both of Jagger's parents — the Rolling Stones lead singer and model Jerry Hall — were in attendance, according to the Daily Mail.

Brianne Howey & Matt Ziering

The Ginny & Georgia actress wed fiancé Matt Ziering on July 24 in the garden of a family home in Palos Verdes, California.

"It was the most beautiful feeling to be surrounded by so many people we love. The night was more magical than I could have ever imagined," Howey told PEOPLE.

"I wish all of my nights could end with a Son of a Gun fried chicken sandwich and a Spice Girls dance party," she added of the event, which was attended by just over 100 guests.

The 30-year-old niece of Princess Diana wed the millionaire, 62, in front of friends and family at a luxurious ceremony over the July 24 weekend at Villa Aldo Brandini in Frascati, Italy.

Joining Kitty on her special day were her brothers Louis Spencer, Viscount Althorp and Samuel Aitken walked her down the aisle, according to Daily Mail.

Jenna Uskowitz & David Stanley

The Glee star and Stanley wed on July 24 in an outdoor ceremony in a Los Angeles, with Brides getting an exclusive first look at the romantic nuptials — and their gorgeous wedding portrait.

"We were elated and so grateful that we didn't have to push our wedding and that we got to have the day of our dreams," the actress told Brides. "It was such a gift that we were able to share it with our friends and family."

For her special day, Ushkowitz wore an elegant strapless gown by Netta BenShabu and Kinsley James Couture Bridal, with Stanley opting for a tux from Hugo Boss.

Carson Boatman & Julana Dizon

Days of Our Lives actor Boatman is officially a married man.

Boatman, 27, wed model Dizon on July 23 at a ceremony in Des Moines, Iowa, near where he grew up.

The happy couple first met in 2016 in Los Angeles, when their mutual gym trainer played "matchmaker" and set them up for a dinner date. "And the rest is history," Boatman says.

Ellie Monahan & Mark Dobrosky

Katie Couric's daughter Ellie Monahan said "I do" with a bang!

Monahan tied the knot with Dobrosky on July 4 at Cedar Lakes Estate in Port Jervis, New York.

Looking back at her wedding, she told PEOPLE that between the delicious food, unforgettable music and heartfelt speeches from family members, there are "too many" special moments to count.

Bobby Bones & Caitlin Parker

The radio host and Parker said "I do" in front of their loved ones and at their Nashville-area home on July 17.

"We love home. We picked this place out together — that was really one of the first decisions that we made as a couple. And so she thought, 'What if we got married here?'" Bones, 41, told PEOPLE exclusively of deciding to hold the wedding in their backyard.

The couple, who were introduced through mutual friends and began dating a few months before the pandemic hit in March 2020, wrote their own vows and were married by Parker's childhood music minister, Jeff Elkins.

Music industry guests included country stars Dierks Bentley, Jake Owen, Chuck Wicks and radio personality Charlamagne Tha God, as well as Dan + Shay, who performed for the couple's first dance.

Derek Fisher & Gloria Govan

After a three-year engagement, the former NBA player wed Govan on July 17 at Cielo Farms in Malibu, California. The pair, who began dating in 2015, pushed back their April 2020 wedding one year due to the coronavirus pandemic.

"We got engaged in 2018, and we eagerly started planning the wedding, but, unfortunately, like many, we had to cancel our big day," Govan, 36, told PEOPLE. "There were times we thought we should have a Zoom wedding instead of waiting but quickly realized our families being a part of the big day was important and our kids, who are just as excited as we are."

Family played a large role in the special day, with Govan's brother Eric officiating the ceremony.

"After almost two years of waiting here, we are — and we couldn't be happier," said the bride.

Naomi Cooke & Martin Johnson

The Runaway June singer-songwriter said "I do" to Martin Johnson, lead singer of The Night Game and Boys Like Girls at the Bald Mountain Camps Resort in Rangeley, Maine.

"We got married!!!" Cooke, 31, revealed on Instagram on July 13. "Over the weekend a small but might fortress of friends and family held hands in a circle around us as we turned the page into a brand new chapter of life as husband and wife."
