Sutton stabbing: Teenage boy, 17, dies after bloody knife attack near train station as police launch

September 2024 · 2 minute read

COPS have launched an urgent manhunt after a teenage boy was stabbed to death.

The horror attack took place by Sutton High Street station at 7pm this evening.

Police are now searching for a suspect who fled the scene.

A police spokesperson said: “Police and LAS were called to Sutton High Street at about 7.08pm on Tuesday December 5, following reports that a teenager had been stabbed.

"On arrival officers found a 17-year-old male suffering with stab wounds.

"He was treated at the scene but sadly died as a result of his injuries.

"Enquiries are ongoing to trace his next of kin. No arrests have been made."

The stabbing comes just half an hour after a woman was shot in Hackney, east London.

A Scotland Yard statement said: "A murder investigation is under way after a woman died in a shooting in Hackney.

"Police were called at 18:28hrs on Tuesday December 5 to reports of a shooting in Vine Close, E5.
