Hull City boss Marco Silva and Swansea City manager Paul Clement both have tough jobs on their hand,

May 2024 · 4 minute read

HULL’S Marco Silva won the battle of the new bosses against Swansea’s Paul Clement in the FA Cup.

But which manager has the best chance of steering their struggling side to Premier League safety?

EXPERIENCE

SILVA: Saturday was Silva’s first taste of English football.

Keep up to date with ALL the Hull City and Swansea City news, gossip, transfers and goals on our club page plus fixtures, results and live match commentary.

But despite being only 39, this is the Portuguese’s sixth year in management, having enjoyed successful spells at Estoril and Sporting  Lisbon in his homeland and Olympiakos in Greece.

It helps that Silva’s English is good. But he is no Sam Allardyce, with this his first survival scrap. 4/10

Related stories

CLEMENT: There is no questioning Clement’s expertise as a coach or his knowledge of the English game.

Yet his only previous post as a manager was with Derby last season.

And, however harsh his sacking seemed at the time, he only lasted 33 games.

Bossing a promotion-chasing Championship side is also very different to taking over a team fighting for their lives in the Prem. 4/10

CONFIDENCE

SILVA:  At his unveiling, the new Hull boss admitted his team might need a “miracle” to stay up.

But he repeated the phrase “I believe” on numerous occasions.

And winning his first match in charge — and Hull’s first in eight — will give him and his players more reason to do so.

Local lad Josh Tymon, 17, who scored his side’s 90th- minute second, admitted: “He has put his spin on how he wants us to play.” 7/10

CLEMENT:  He would not have quit his cushy gig at Bayern Munich had he thought the Swans were a lost cause.

This defeat will also not knock his belief, given his much-changed side performed well for large parts.

Keeper Kristoffer Nordfeldt said: “Even though he has only been at the club for a few days, he has had a positive impact.”

But Clement would not be human if there was not some self-doubt, given his rather blank CV as a boss. 6/10

SQUAD

SILVA:  Eleven players are currently missing, with captain Michael Dawson the latest casualty, picking up a shoulder injury on Saturday.

But Abel Hernandez has returned from two months out, coming off the bench to score his first goal since August against the Swans.

And if Silva can get last season’s 21-goal hitman firing, it would solve a major problem for the Prem’s joint-lowest scorers.

Silva said: “Hernandez can be important.

“He is a different player in our attack line and he is a quality player. We will see if he can make the difference.” 3/10

CLEMENT: With no African Nations Cup absentees and only two players injured, Clement could consider himself fortunate.

He definitely has some quality to work with — Gylfi Sigurdsson would walk into most mid-table sides and an in-form Fernando Llorente is a fine option up front.

Clement will need to do something with a defence that has let in a league-high 45 goals. But left-back Stephen Kingsley added: “We are excited about what’s coming up. I think we’ll improve.” 4/10

TRANSFER WINDOW PROSPECTS

SILVA:  Hull desperately need reinforcements and Silva says he is already working on targets.

But with the Allam family wanting to sell, he added: “We don’t have a lot of money to spend.”

So this month may be spent trying to keep hold of prize assets like Robert Snodgrass. 3/10

CLEMENT: Chairman Huw Jenkins said last month that Swansea’s American owners are “fully committed to make some signings”.

And upon his unveiling, Clement said he had received “assurances” over spending, with a defender no doubt his priority.

On Saturday, he again confirmed he will be active in the market to bring in “better” players.

But Clement would not comment on Chelsea’s interest in Spanish hitman Llorente, a man he will be desperate to keep hold of. 5/10

VERDICT

Both bosses are big gambles for their respective clubs — for  different reasons.

But Clement has a slight edge given he has more players available and more money to spend this month.

SILVA: 17/40 CLEMENT: 19/40
