Wesley Robert Edens, born October 30, 1961, is an American billionaire businessman.

He is popularly known for being the founder of Fortress Investment Group, New Fortress Energy and co-owner of the Milwaukee Bucks of the NBA.

Moreover, the Montana-born businessman also owns the Premier League soccer team Aston Villa.

A quick background check: His father worked as a psychologist, and his mother was a teacher.

As a teenager, Wesley was a competitive skier and still likes to scale mountains worldwide.

Before founding his investment group, Edens worked as a partner at Lehman Brothers and BlackRock.

Wesley and Marc Lasry purchased the NBA team Milwaukee Bucks in 2014 for $550 million.

Moreover, Edens also funded the construction of a $512 million basketball arena, Fiserv Forum, which has luxury club suites, concessions from local vendors and a plaza with a brewery and sports bar.

Besides, the billionaire with a net worth of $3.6 billion had someone who stood by him through all thicks and thins; his wife.

Wesley shared his wedding vows with Lynn Edens. Born Lynn Marie Bartholomew on December 20, 1963, she is the daughter of John Bartholomew and Carol Dier.

Reportedly, Lynn raises alpacas and sells their fleece at Little Creek Farm Alpacas. However, she previously worked as a bond market analyst on Wall Street for over a decade.
