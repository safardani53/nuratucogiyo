The 16 Best Laundry Hampers of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

February 2024 · 16 minute read

Everyone does laundry — apparently even Kim Kardashian — and the first step is rounding up your dirty clothes. If you're in the market for a new hamper or basket, we've got you covered. But which one should you choose?

"It really depends on your habits, so be mindful of where you set clothes on a daily basis," says professional organizer Laura Bostrom of Everyday Order. She recommends hampers made of soft materials (or at least with soft linings) that won't snag your fabrics. They should also be easy to move around and, ideally, stylish.

To help you narrow down your choices, we rounded up functional, durable, aesthetically pleasing options of various sizes and styles. We also put some of our favorite laundry baskets and laundry hampers to the test to see just how easily we could carry or maneuver each one from the bedroom to the laundry room.  From lidded woven containers and braided baskets to collapsible, rolling, and easy-sorting models, you'll find it all here.

Check out the best laundry hampers and baskets below.

How to Pick the Right Laundry Hamper

Get the right size

The first thing to consider when buying a laundry hamper is the size. It should hold about a week's worth of dirty clothes, if not more. Couples and those who like to pre-sort their darks and lights might want to look for something with two or more compartments, such as the Hennez Laundry Basket With Lid. 

Look for durability

A durable design is vital. Your laundry hamper should be made of sturdy materials, so it stays upright when stuffed full of clothes. Be sure to note the weight capacity before buying one. Depending on the material, some large models may not support as much weight as you'd think. Plastic tends to be a pretty sturdy material that’s commonly used in laundry baskets and hampers, but it also might not be as visually appealing as materials like bamboo or cloth.

Consider your home decor

Last but not least, you'll want to look for a hamper that complements your home design. This may not be as important for one that's concealed in a closet or laundry room, but ideally, the material, color, and style should mesh with the rest of your decor. If minimalist neutrals are your vibe, then our best overall pick from Pottery Barn would be a perfect addition to your room decor.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What material is best for a laundry hamper?

    "Hampers should be a soft material or have a lining that won't snag clothing," says Bostrom — or other items like silk and satin sheets. If the primary material isn't particularly soft (such as rattan, bamboo, or jute), look for one with a fabric liner. "It also needs to be light enough to carry to the laundry space."

  • What is the difference between a laundry basket and a laundry hamper?

    Some people use the terms interchangeably. However, a laundry basket is generally for transporting laundry from one place to another, such as bringing clean clothes from the dryer over to the folding area. A hamper, on the other hand, is for collecting dirty clothes and household linens until it's time to wash them.

  • Where is the best place to put a laundry hamper?

    Bostrom recommends thinking about where you typically undress at the end of the day, whether it's in your closet, bedroom, or bathroom. "I switched things up and moved our hamper from the bedroom to the bathroom," she says. "This created a better flow for our lifestyle and habits — and fewer piles of clothes on the floor."
