Zanussi oven 'explodes' scattering shattered glass everywhere in mum's kitchen minutes after teen so

September 2024 Â· 3 minute read

A SHOCKED mum whose kitchen was covered in glass after her oven exploded warns "it could be deadly" for others.

She says it was switched off after being used by her teenage son, minutes before the glass panel shattered.

Paula Chandler and her family were in the other room when they heard "a massive bang" from the Zanussi oven.

The 33-year-old from Thamesmead, south London, said her 13-year-old son had been cooking in the kitchen just minutes before she says glass was sent flying everywhere.

The mum-of-two said: "It was about nine o'clock at night there was myself and my friend in the house then. We had our kids, and it was my teenage son, there's a baby of two months and then two toddlers of two years.

"We was all just in the living room we heard a massive bang like an explosion.

"We was asking what could it have been and we thought someone had left a pan on and it had exploded.

"When we opened the kitchen door the cooker had exploded everywhere.

"The whole glass off the front part had shattered.

"There was a lot of glass all over the place, on the floor there was a lot of pieces.

"We couldn't figure out what had happened as it was switched off and switched off from the main.

"The oven had been on minutes before. It was very lucky he [her son] wasn't in the room.

"Had anyone been in the kitchen at the time there definitely would've been a hospital trip. Someone could have been killed.

She said there had been baby clothes hanging in the kitchen at the time of the explosion, which she was forced to throw away incase glass had become embedded in them.

Paula added marks were left on the floor as the shards were so hot, made a complaint with Zanussi and she had commented on the company's Facebook page but not had a response.

She said she had found online examples of other Zanussi products which had also exploded and now wants to warn others.

The mum said: "These are not safe products, This could happen to anyone at any time and it could be deadly."

A spokeswoman for Zanussi said after speaking with Paula the oven had been collected by her landlord.

She added they need specific information, and to collect the oven if possible, to investigate further.

She said they have been unable to reach Paula since that conversation to get hold of that information.

Adding: "We are still awaiting information and have asked Miss Chandler to contact us via direct dial or email. As you can imagine it is our upmost urgency to find out what has happened here.

"At the time of sending this update (16th Feb 7pm) we are awaiting contact from Miss Chandler.

"We are not able to comment on the glass until we have had the opportunity to gain access to the product."
