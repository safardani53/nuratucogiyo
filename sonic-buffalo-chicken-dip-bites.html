Sonic 'reimagines' an on-the-go, fan-favorite meal for under $3 - but there's a catch

August 2024 · 4 minute read

SONIC is launching a brand-new summer item that’s perfect for any tailgate, and the meal is less than $3.

As Sonic approaches the hectic back-to-school and fall football season, the drive-thru fast food joint has unveiled its own twist on a classic appetizer.

Sonic’s new Buffalo Chicken Dip Bites will be available for a limited time, and customers can’t way to try the creative new menu item.

Each bite packs in all the flavor of a buffalo chicken dip with a wonton-style fried exterior “to deliver the ultimate crispy bite that’s packed with creamy, craveable goodness,” the company said in a press release.

Executives said the restaurant designed the new entree to be a perfect pairing for any family or friend gathering.

“From tailgates to family celebrations, Buffalo chicken dip is always a craveable favorite,” said Mackenzie Gibson, Sonic’s vice president of culinary & menu innovation in a statement.

“In developing the new Buffalo Chicken Dip Bites, our Culinary Innovation team took that same mouthwatering flavor and packed it into a golden-brown crispy wrapper for an unforgettable snacking experience that can be enjoyed anywhere, anytime.”

Each bite is mix of juicy chicken, zesty buffalo sauce and melted cheddar cheese packed inside of the crispy wonton dumpling.

The Buffalo Chicken Dip Bites can be eaten alone or brought to a party for mutual enjoyment.

For three pieces, Sonic sells the bites for $2.99, but you can also purchase a pack of five or seven.

Fans have already been praising the new item on social media.

 “It really tasted like buffalo chicken dip in a little ravioli,” TikToker @theshayspence wrote.

Another user said: “Looks like a Buffalo chicken deep fried pierogi.”

Meanwhile, a Reddit review also said the dish, which works as either an entree or a side, was a strong option on Sonic’s menu.

“I tried these for lunch today and they were fantastic,” they wrote.

“Crispy, almost wonton-like exterior, mildly spicy buffalo-sauced shredded chicken inside. Overall, each bite is fairly small so it makes more of a side than a main unless you order the largest size, but they're delicious.”

The only con of the new item is its limited-time status, so Sonic fans should rush to try the chicken treat before it disappears for good.

Also new on Sonic’s lineup this summer is a special new shake deal.

For a limited time, all small Classic Shakes will be available for just $1.99.

Customers can take advantage of this deal all throughout August.

Shakes come hand mixed in a range of flavors: chocolate, Reese’s peanut butter, banana or strawberry.

And all drink, slush and shake sales will donate a portion to support Sonic’s Limeades for Learning program.

Historically, Sonic has used the initiative to donate more than $26million to classrooms all across the United States.

SONIC IN THE NEWS

Sonic also teamed up with several celebrities to promote its new summer drink collection.

Through the initiative, customers can sip like their favorite stars or choose from over 100 customizable options to create their own “remix” drink.

By “remixing your drink,” guests now have the chance to order a base soda, slush, or lemonade, and then choose from various flavor add-ins.

These include options like cranberry and sugar-free mango flavors, real fruit such as cherries and lemons and even NERDS Candy.

But in what’s been less exciting news for Sonic fans, customers were alerted that the iconic Shark Week Slush will not come back to the menu this summer.

The Shark Week Slush featured icy blue coconut slush with a layer of real strawberries and shark gummy candies.

OTHER NEW ITEMS

As Americans continue to look for summer flavors, many other restaurants have taken advantage of the season as a way to unveil new menu additions.

Burger King launched two new twists on its classic Chicken Fries, and now customers can dial up the seasoning.

Now the burger giant will sell its chicken parm and chicken & waffle fries flavors.

The two new spins on the chicken fries will only be available for a limited time for customers in the Boston, Massachusetts area, Burger King confirmed to The U.S. Sun.

Meanwhile, Taco Bell has relaunched the formerly discontinued beefy crunch burrito.

The entree makes it way back to menus on August 3.

Each burrito consists of ground beef, rice, and cheese inside a tortilla stuffed with Frito-Lay's Hot Fritos.

Taco Bell also unveiled its own take on Birria tacos.

The chain’s new specialty tacos are filled with slow braised beef and served with a side of savory stew to dip into.

At Taco Bell, two dipping sauces are currently available: nacho cheese and the classic red sauce.
