Ashlee Evans Smith: Early Life & Family

Ashlee Evans-Smith was born to her father, Tim Evans, and an unknown mother on July 9, 1987, in Ukiah, California. But, unfortunately, that is all we could find despite working for hours.

Added to that, Evans-Smith does not seem to have any siblings.  At least, that’s what her Instagram account portrays because we did not see any posts related to her siblings.

The 5 feet 8 brunettes attended North Monterey County for her high school, continuing her education. After that, Ashlee graduated from Menlo College, located in California.

During her time at Menlo, the 30-year-old took part in wrestling programs that paved her a route into MMA later on.

Ashlee Evans Smith: Career (UFC)

Before making her professional debut, Ashlee fought as an amateur for the Tuff-N-Fuff organization for two years. As an amateur, the 30-year-old held a record of five wins and four losses before making her professional debut in 2013.

She began her professional career on March 2, 2013, fighting for the CFAs CFA Women’s PGA Tournament.

There Evans-Smith defeated Tori Adams via a unanimous decision after dominating the three-round bout on her debut.

After that, for the semi-final round at CFA 11, she was scheduled to fight with Peggy Morgan, but after auditioning for TUF 18, she was removed from the card.

However, she regained her place later on after becoming the youngest player selected for TUF 18.

Luckily, she reached the finals as Morgan was removed from the tournaments. Ashlee later faced fighter Fallon Fox, one of the American MMA fighters. In the match against Fox, Evans won the third round.

Smith then signed with the World Series of Fighting, where she fought against Marciea Allen at WSOF 10 and won the match via TKOs.

As a result of her outstanding performances, UFC signed the talented fighter in 2014. Subsequently, Ashlee was supposed to face the woman who would later be the first to beat Ronda Rousey, Holly Holm. 

But, sadly, Holm got injured and had to be replaced.

Ashlee Evans Smith vs. Raquel Pennington

Consequently, another rising star Raquel Pennington stepped in and fought Ashlee. For the most part, the bout was highly balanced until Pennington secured a bulldog choke and won.

After that, Ashlee-Smith bounced back and won her next fights against Marion Reneau and Veronica Macedo.

Consequently, it seemed like the California native had finally settled into her new environment.

On the contrary, Ashlee went on a two-fight losing streak against Ketlen Vieira and Sarah Moras before defeating Bec Rawlings at UFC 223.

Finally, the 36-year-old faced Andrea Lee for her most recent bout, who was defeated via a unanimous decision.

After that, Evans-Smith was supposed to face Molly McCann on March 21, 2020. However, the bout got canceled due to the world pandemic of the Corona Virus (COVID-19).

Right after the pandemic was under control, Evans faced Norma Dumont at UFC on ESPN: Blaydes vs. Lewis. However, she lost the fight via a unanimous decision.

Ashlee Evans Smith: Stealing Medical Supplies

Ashlee made the headlines for all the wrong reasons in March 2020. To illustrate, she was caught stealing medical supplies, which were meant to be donated for the purposes of the Corona Virus’ treatment.

According to reports, the 36-year-old and her boyfriend took off with the medical supplies from individuals planning to donate the items to the local hospital.

As a result, the individual in question, Selina Cohen, demanded that her supplies be returned.

However, Ashlee refused to answer Selina, which prompted a back and forth between them on social media platforms.

Later on, Evans-Smith posted an Instagram story explaining her side of the story, which got deleted soon after.
