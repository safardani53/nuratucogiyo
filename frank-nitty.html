Frank Nitty | Early Life, Education, & Family

Frank grew up in the Watts area of L.A. and attended Jordan High School.

He is 1.88 m tall, weighs 175 lb, and has a tall and slender body.

Involved in skateboarding, he did not play basketball for Jordan until his junior year, when the team’s head coach, Van Myers, considered his raw athleticism.

He attended David Starr Jordan High School in L.A., California, before enrolling at Irvine Valley College and Saddleback College. Later he joined Weber State and Cal State University.

There is no report about his parents and siblings. Frank’s birth sign is Aquarius. He refers to Afro-American ethnicity. 

Frank Nitty | From Cellphone Salesman To Basketball League Legend

College Stats & Career

During college, he began his basketball career at Irvine Valley College. Meanwhile, his rookie season equated to 14.5 points, 10.5 rebounds, and three assists while assisting his team in a conference championship.

After a year at Irvine Valley, he went to Saddleback College. He struck for one year and was named All-California Community College first team and MVP of the Orange Empire Conference.

Frank averaged 13.3 rebounds per contest in 3 games at the state tournament.

Later one season at Saddleback, he went to Weber State for his junior season. He rose in the same backcourt as likely NBA All-Star Damian Lillard.

Furthermore, he scored 10.2 points (3rd on the team), six rebounds, 2.9 assists, and 1.9 steal into the season, equating to 11.9 points and 6.2 rebounds in 16 conference play.

In 2010, Frank was named the Big Sky Conference Newcomer of the Year and the second-team All-Big Sky. However, he was ousted from the team after the end of the season.

Moreover, he played his senior season at Division II Cal State, L.A., where he recorded 16.3 points, 7.6 rebounds, and a conference-leading 2.6 steals per game and was named first-team All-CCAA.

Also, he assisted in leading the Golden Eagles to their first-ever CCAA Championship Tournament win.

Professional Career

Frank was initially chosen in the 6th round of the 2011 NBA Development League Draft by the Los Angeles D-Fenders. However, it was postponed due to injury after playing only two games.

Frank then chose not to seek a professional basketball career and ultimately became the Verizon Wireless store manager.

Frank began playing in other leagues around Los Angeles before finally competing in the Drew League.

Many NBA players compete in this pro-am summer exhibition league, Including Frank using his nickname “Frank Nitty.”

After the successful Drew League performance, Frank was signed by the South Bay Lakers of the NBA D League. Throughout the season with South Bay, he was among the team’s final cuts.

He was titled the Drew League’s MVP in 2016 and 2017, while his team, Birdie’s Revenge, won the Drew League championship in the later year.

On November 9, 2017, he was signed with the Island Storm of the National Basketball League of Canada (NBLC).

After 2018

Frank earned strong attention for scoring 44 points on Chicago Bulls player Denzel Valentine and winning his third MVP in 2018.

On September 28, 2018, he signed with Al-Gharafa of the Qatari Basketball League.

His record was 22.8 points, 10.9 rebounds, and 7.5 assists over the 17-game regular season, with the missed playoffs of a 7–9 record.

Nitty played for the team Public Enemy in the Drew League and was the Drew League Defensive Player of the Year in 2019.

Later for the 2019–20 season, he signed with Jeoutai Basketball of the Taiwanese Super Basketball League (SBL).

Frank signed a contract with Al Gharafa for his second stint on November 24, 2020, and is still with them in 2023.

The BIG3

After getting selected for BIG3, Frank crossed the BIG3 threshold and took over the captaincy of Killer 3’s in 2021.

Frank was in the third category of BIG3, averaging 7.8 points per game with 25 assists. He also led with 11 steals and a monster dunk against Aliens in week three of 2021.

Killer 3s for Game 4 in week 5 competed against Enemies at the BIG3 league in 2022.

Killer3’s was the winner; Coach Charles Oakley and Captain Frank Nitty’s team were on fire, constantly putting pressure on the Enemies.
