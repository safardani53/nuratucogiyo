Where is Let's Make a Deal filmed?

October 2024 · 2 minute read

POPULAR TV game show Let's Make A Deal has been entertaining audiences and at-home viewers since 1963.

The long-running show challenges contestants to make smart trading moves to bring home the most valuable prize.

What is Let’s Make a Deal about?

Popularized by Monty Hall in the 1960s, Let's Make a Deal is a show all about trading and strategic deal-making.

It involves a series of games where contestants, also called "traders," are asked to trade a smaller prize for a mystery prize.

There's a wide range of prizes available, including cash, cars, electronics, furniture, and all-expense paid trips.

The mystery prize participants can trade for can either be a less valuable item, something of higher value, or a "zonk" or a gag item.

The traders are also given the option to keep what they have.

Who is the host of Let’s Make a Deal?

Let's Make a Deal is currently hosted by comedian Wayne Brady.

The actor also happens to be the host of another game show, Game of Talents, which airs on Fox.

He told the New York Post that while hosting two games shows on competing networks is almost unheard of, his setup is relatively conflict-free.

“While it’s not the norm it’s not completely unorthodox,” he said.

"Even though I’m hosting a show on a rival network, CBS understands I’m not just a host and I just don’t do one thing and they’ve been very good about that.

“And Let’s Make a Deal is in daytime and [Game of Talents] is primetime so they’re not in direct competition."

Previous Let's Make a Deal hosts include Monty Hall, Bob Hilton, and Billy Bush.

Where is Let’s Make a Deal filmed?

Let's Make a Deal is filmed at Haven Studios in Glendale, California, along with the other Fremantle game show, The Price is Right.

Previously, the series was taped at Quixote Studios in Sylmar, California.

Past filming locations also include Saticoy Studios in Van Nuys, California, and the Tropicana in Las Vegas.

When the show was first launched in 1963, it was filmed at the NBC Studios in Burbank, California.
